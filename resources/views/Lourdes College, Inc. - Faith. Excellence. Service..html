Lourdes College BSHM Students Excel in US Work and Travel Program

Two (2) BSHM 4th year students, Devid Leif M. Carbajal and Pomela Stephan D. Fuentes successfully completed their journey in the US Work and Travel Program at Captiva Dr. Florida and Pembroke, Virginia last May 2024 until August 2024.

According to Carbajal, “One of the most significant lessons I’ve learned was the importance of teamwork and effective communication.” It was on May 27, 2024 when he started his journey with full commitment which then ended on August 18, 2024. On the other hand, Fuentes started her work and travel program on May 26, 2024, and ended on August 7, 2024.

Fuentes said that, “One thing [that] I learned during the program which I always put in my mind is to always do it for yourself and believe in yourself because all experiences that I went through during the program, was an opportunity given to me to learn from it.”

This milestone was a great achievement that strengthened and shaped Carbajal and Fuentes in applying their skills and knowledge in the world of work. The opportunity allowed the students to fully immerse themselves in their chosen industry while also learning it in a different culture and country.

Lourdes College Seals Partnership with Big Ben Music Indonesia

As part of its dedication to providing academic excellence, Lourdes College proudly announced a noteworthy partnership with Big Ben Music Indonesia on November 5, 2024.

Big Ben Music Indonesia is an international academy for Music, English, and Performing Arts.  The institution aims to provide students not just with a structured approach to gradually acquire the skills and knowledge required to play an instrument, sing, and confidently communicate in English, but also to become competent performers.

This partnership will open up a new learning path for Lourdesians, specifically in the Bachelor of Music, BSED-English, and Bachelor of Culture and Arts Education programs. 

Students will have an array of knowledge and access to a cutting-edge teaching system created by experts from London West University. This opportunity at hand offers a landscape for the development of skilled musicians, educators, and artists to thrive in their specialized fields.

This partnership demonstrates Lourdes College’s commitment to offering valuable educational experiences while fostering its students’ skills and aspirations. With a common goal of excellence, both schools anticipate a successful future.

Ignacian-Marian Oro Scholars Shine at Kinanao Awards 2024

The Cagayan de Oro City Scholarships Office honored exceptional organizations and city scholars dedicated to community service during the prestigious awarding ceremony at the City Tourism Hall on July 26, 2024. Among the recipients was Lourdes College’s Ignacian Marian Oro Scholars (IMOS), proudly recognized as the Most Outstanding School-Based Organization at the esteemed Kinanao Awards 2024. This recognition highlights IMOS’ unwavering commitment to service and excellence, showcasing the dedication of its members to making a meaningful impact in the community.

In addition to the organization’s top honor, several IMOS members were individually recognized for their outstanding contributions. Mr. Ray Oliver Sab Amante received the Exemplar Award, while the Service Awards went to Ms. Kianna Therese Andrada, Ms. Wyeth Jean Tresares, Ms. Nicole Cual, Mr. Ray Oliver Amante, Ms. Reyzel Tabios, Mr. Erven Granada, and Mr. Jason Ramiro. Furthermore, Ms. Wyeth Jean Tresares and Ms. Nicole Cual were named Finalists for the Volunteer of the Year award, recognizing their exceptional dedication to volunteer work and community service.

LC Learning Commons Coordinator Receives Accolade in PRC

During the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) awards, Mrs. Annabelle P. Acedera, Learning Commons Coordinator, was awarded as the 2024 Outstanding Professional of the Year in the Librarianship field. The awarding ceremony was held at Forbes Ballroom, Conrad Manila, Pasay City, last August 2, 2024 with the theme “Bagong Pilipinas: Pagpupugay sa Makabago at Mapanlikhang Propesyonal ng Bayan”.

This prestigious national event recognizes professionals who exemplify expertise, proficiency, integrity, and unwavering dedication to their craft, as endorsed by their peers. Mrs. Acedera’s recognition reflects not only her exceptional contributions to the field of librarianship but also her dedication to innovation and lifelong learning, which have positively impacted the academic community she serves.

As an academic institution that promotes an ethos of academic excellence and multifaceted growth, Lourdes College takes immense pride in celebrating Dr. Acedera’s remarkable achievement. This spectacular feat and commitment truly brought an honor and will serve as an inspiration to future librarians.

About Us

Learn about our vision, mission, and policies shaping academic excellence.

Vision

In our Education Ministry, we witness to the loving compassion of Jesus, the Teacher. True to our Ignacian Marian identity, we empower and nurture learning communities towards ethical and corporate responsibility for inner and social transformation for the common good.

Mission

We commit ourselves to: 

  1. Grow deeper in discernment and interior freedom to be prophets of hope in today’s world;
  2. Continuously form Ignacian Marian leaders who witness to faith, excellence and service in varied socio-cultural settings;
  3. Constantly pursue innovative programs, approaches, and educational strategies to develop world-class professionals;
  4. Build up resources and capabilities to respond to  contemporary issues towards enhancement of quality of life; and
  5. Expand our educational thrust for the poor.

Educational Organization Policy

We, at Lourdes College Inc. of Cagayan de Oro City, provide inclusive and transformative Ignacian Marian education to produce service-oriented individuals and world-class professionals who serve for the common good.

Guided by the principles of Educational Organization Management System, we commit to faithfully comply with all the applicable external and internal requirements and sustain our educational management system. 

Together, we embrace the highest educational quality standards through continuous innovation of instructional and support processes, research, community engagement & services, aligned with sustainable development goals, stakeholders’ needs and expectations, and ethical and responsible use of technology.

Key Directions

  1. Transformative Ignacian Marian Education towards service for the common good.
  2. Strong and relevant Institutional programs to enhance quality of life.
  3. Responsible partnership to sustain and advance human welfare.
  4. Sustainable programs to favor the disadvantaged sectors of the society.

Privacy Policy

We from Lourdes College of Cagayan de Oro City make sure that all of the personal information you have provided are secured and confidential. With your consent, we collect the necessary personal information with the intent to fulfill its purpose.
